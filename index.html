The Dokkōdō for Futures Trading

21 Precepts for the Way of the Trader

The Way of the Trader

Adapted from Miyamoto Musashi's Dokkōdō (The Way of Walking Alone). These 21 precepts guide traders toward mastery through self-reliance, emotional detachment, and commitment to proven principles.

1

Accept market conditions as they are

Accept market conditions as they are, not as you wish them to be. Don't fight trends or impose bias on the market.

2

Do not trade for pleasure alone

Don't trade for thrills. Avoid revenge trading. Trade only when your edge is present.

3

Never depend on partial analysis

Never base trades on hunches or incomplete analysis. Wait for your full setup to materialize.

4

Think humbly, study deeply

Stay humble about your abilities while deeply studying market dynamics and global events.

5

Be detached from outcomes

Release attachment to specific outcomes. Don't fall in love with positions. Focus on process over profits.

6

Do not regret past trades

Don't dwell on losses or missed opportunities. Each trade is independent. Learn but don't let regret cloud judgment.

7

Never envy other traders

Don't envy others' success or compare performance. Focus on your own consistent improvement.

8

Never mourn winning streaks

Don't become attached to winning streaks or strategies. Adapt when circumstances change.

9

Take full responsibility

Don't blame markets, brokers, or external factors. Take full responsibility for all trading decisions.

10

Resist greed and fear

Don't let greed drive position sizing or fear prevent valid setups. Trade with discipline.

11

Have no directional bias

Trade long or short based on what the market presents. Don't favor directions based on personal bias.

12

Adapt to any environment

Adapt to trending, ranging, volatile, or quiet markets. Don't pine for "better" conditions.

13

Avoid addiction to big wins

Don't become addicted to the high of big wins. Maintain discipline whether winning or losing.

14

Cut what doesn't serve you

Cut losing positions quickly. Don't hold onto outdated strategies or beliefs.

15

Think independently

Don't follow crowd sentiment or gurus. Test conventional wisdom yourself before accepting it.

16

Master few tools

Don't over-complicate with too many indicators. Master a few reliable methods rather than collecting many.

17

Accept necessary losses

Don't fear losses if they're part of your tested system. Some losses are inevitable and necessary.

18

Build wealth consistently

Don't try to solve financial problems with one trade. Build wealth consistently over time.

19

Respect markets, depend on yourself

Respect forces beyond your control, but don't rely on luck or hope. Depend on skill and preparation.

20

Preserve your principles

Accept losses and drawdowns, but never compromise trading principles or risk management rules.

21

Never abandon your system

Stay committed to your proven method. Don't abandon your system during difficult periods.
