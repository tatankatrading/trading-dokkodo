The Dokkodo

21 Precepts for the Way of the Trader

1

Accept market conditions as they are

Accept market conditions as they are, not as you wish them to be. Don't fight trends, don't argue with price action, and don't impose your bias on the market.

2

Do not trade for pleasure alone

Don't trade for the thrill or excitement. Avoid revenge trading or gambling behaviors. Trade only when your edge is present, not for entertainment.

3

Never depend on partial analysis

Never base trades on hunches, emotions, or incomplete analysis. Wait for your full trading setup to materialize before entering positions.

4

Think humbly of your abilities and deeply of the markets

Stay humble about your abilities while deeply studying market dynamics, economic factors, and global events that drive futures prices.

5

Be detached from trading outcomes

Release attachment to specific outcomes. Don't fall in love with positions or become emotionally invested in being right. Focus on process over profits.

6

Do not regret past trades

Don't dwell on past losses or missed opportunities. Each trade is independent. Learn from mistakes but don't let regret cloud future decision-making.

7

Never envy other traders

Don't envy other traders' success or compare your performance to others. Focus on your own consistent improvement and edge development.

8

Never mourn the end of winning streaks

Don't become attached to winning streaks, favorite strategies, or market conditions. Adapt when circumstances change.

9

Take responsibility for all trading outcomes

Don't blame the market, brokers, news, or external factors for losses. Take full responsibility for your trading decisions and outcomes.

10

Do not let greed or fear guide you

Don't let greed drive position sizing or hold winners too long hoping for more. Don't let fear prevent you from taking valid setups.

11

Have no directional bias

Be willing to trade long or short based on what the market presents. Don't favor certain commodities or directions based on personal bias.

12

Adapt to any market environment

Adapt your trading to any market environment - trending, ranging, volatile, or quiet. Don't pine for "better" market conditions.

13

Do not become addicted to big wins

Don't become addicted to the "high" of big wins. Maintain the same discipline and process whether you're winning or losing.

14

Cut what no longer serves you

Cut losing positions quickly. Don't hold onto outdated strategies, indicators, or beliefs that no longer serve your trading.

15

Think independently of the crowd

Think independently. Don't follow crowd sentiment, popular trading gurus, or conventional wisdom without testing it yourself.

16

Master few tools rather than collect many

Don't over-complicate your trading with too many indicators, strategies, or tools. Master a few reliable methods rather than collecting many.

17

Do not fear necessary losses

Don't fear losses or drawdowns if they're part of your tested system. Accept that some losses are inevitable and necessary for long-term success.

18

Build wealth consistently over time

Don't try to make one trade solve all your financial problems. Build wealth consistently over time rather than seeking home runs.

19

Respect the market but depend on yourself

Respect the market and acknowledge forces beyond your control, but don't rely on luck, hope, or external salvation. Depend on your skill and preparation.

20

Preserve your trading principles above all

You may accept losses and drawdowns, but never compromise your trading principles, risk management rules, or integrity.

21

Never abandon your proven system

Stay committed to your proven trading method and discipline. Don't abandon your system during difficult periods or chase new strategies constantly.

The Essence of Trading Mastery

Emotional Detachment From outcomes while maintaining disciplined execution
Market Acceptance Of reality without fighting or hoping
Self-Reliance Through independent analysis and decision-making
Simplicity In approach rather than complexity
Consistency In following proven methods
Responsibility For all trading decisions and outcomes
